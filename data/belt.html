ASTEROIDS AND COMETS

Few hundred feet to many miles in diameter

Ceres (asteroid) – one-quarter the diameter of Earth's moon
named a “dwarf planet” in 2006
largest body in the asteroid belt, containing more than 1/3 estimated
total mass of the asteroid belt

Comets contain:
Hydrogen cloud
Nucleus
Coma
As comets near the sun, parts of the nucleus begin to vaporize.
This forms the long tail.

Halley's Comet:
Makes a complete orbit around the sun every 75-76 years.
Visible with the naked eye from Earth.
Named after Edmond Halley, English astronomer.
